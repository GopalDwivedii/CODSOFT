About Marie Curie

Early Life

Born Maria Sk≈Çodowska in Warsaw, Poland, on November 7, 1867, she was the youngest of five children. After her mother's death and financial difficulties, she worked as a governess while secretly attending the underground 'Flying University' where women could study.

Paris Years

In 1891, she moved to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne. Living in poverty, she often forgot to eat while focusing intensely on her studies. She graduated first in her physics degree in 1893.

Meeting Pierre

She met Pierre Curie in 1894, and they married in 1895. Their shared passion for science created one of history's most productive scientific partnerships.

Her Lasting Legacy

Scientific Impact

Her work laid the foundation for modern atomic physics and chemistry. The element curium was named in her honor, and her research led to the development of radiation therapy for cancer treatment.

Breaking Barriers

She broke barriers for women in science, becoming the first female professor at the Sorbonne and inspiring generations of women scientists.

Medical Revolution

Her discovery of radioactivity revolutionized medicine, leading to new treatments for cancer and other diseases.

Research Institutions

The Curie Institutes she founded continue to be world-leading cancer research centers, carrying on her legacy of scientific discovery.
